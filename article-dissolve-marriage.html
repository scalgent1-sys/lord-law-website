How to dissolve a marriage

Marriage concerns are personal, but the legal process must be handled carefully and with proper grounds.

TopicFamily lawForSpouses and families seeking legal clarity

There is no one-size-fits-all answer.

In the Philippines, a person cannot simply end a marriage by agreement. The possible legal routes depend on the facts, the marriage history, and the specific legal grounds that may apply.

Family cases are sensitive because they can affect status, children, property, support, and future plans. The first step is usually to understand which legal remedy fits the facts, if any.

Issues usually reviewed

  • Whether the concern involves nullity, annulment, legal separation, or recognition of foreign divorce.
  • Whether there are children, support, custody, or property concerns.
  • Whether the facts match the grounds required by law.
  • What documents, witnesses, and court steps may be needed.

Why a legal review helps

A legal review helps separate emotional concerns from legal requirements. It can also help the family prepare documents and understand possible timelines before filing anything in court.
