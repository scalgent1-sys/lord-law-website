How to legally terminate an employee

Employee termination should be handled with valid grounds, due process, and proper documentation.

TopicLabor and workplace lawForEmployers, HR teams, and business owners

Termination is not only a business decision.

There are lawful ways to terminate employment in the Philippines, but the employer must be careful. A rushed or undocumented termination can lead to complaints, back wages, damages, or reinstatement issues.

Before ending employment, the company should review the employee's records, company policy, notices, incident reports, and the reason for termination. The safer path is to make the process clear before action is taken.

What employers should review

  • Whether there is a valid cause or authorized cause.
  • Whether notices, hearings, and company policies were followed.
  • Whether the facts are supported by records, warnings, reports, or evidence.
  • Whether final pay, benefits, and required documents are handled properly.

Why documentation matters

Documentation helps show that the employer acted fairly. It also helps the business explain the basis of the decision if the employee later files a labor complaint.
