Can foreigners own real property in the Philippines?

Foreign ownership of Philippine land is limited, so property planning should be checked before money changes hands.

TopicReal estate lawForForeign buyers, families, investors, and property sellers

The general rule is strict.

Foreigners are generally not allowed to own land in the Philippines. This is why real estate transactions involving foreign buyers need careful review before a buyer signs, pays, or transfers funds.

Property transactions can involve title history, tax declarations, deeds, condominium rules, inheritance issues, corporate structures, and registration steps. A buyer should know exactly what right is being acquired and whether the structure is allowed.

Common points to check

  • Whether the property is land, condominium, or another type of right.
  • Whether the buyer is a Filipino citizen, former Filipino citizen, corporation, spouse, heir, or foreign national.
  • Whether the planned ownership structure follows Philippine law.
  • Whether taxes, title issues, and registration steps are clear.

Why early review matters

Early review helps catch restrictions before the parties sign contracts, pay deposits, or file documents. It can also help sellers explain the transaction clearly to buyers.
