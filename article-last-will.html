What is a last will? Is it necessary?

A last will can help a person state how property should be handled after death.

TopicFamily law and estate planningForFamilies, property owners, and business owners

A will is part of planning, not just paperwork.

A last will is a written document where a person gives instructions about property, heirs, and other estate matters. It can reduce confusion, but it must follow legal requirements to be useful.

For families with property, businesses, investments, or complex relationships, planning ahead can reduce stress later. A will can work together with tax planning, property transfer planning, and business succession planning.

Why people prepare one

  • To make wishes clear while the person can still decide.
  • To reduce conflict among family members.
  • To plan for property, business shares, and important assets.
  • To guide the family on what should happen next.

What a family may need to review

Families may need to review property titles, bank accounts, business interests, debts, heirs, special family circumstances, and the documents needed to settle the estate.
